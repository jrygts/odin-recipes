Description

The carne asada is the ultimate celebration of bold, smoky flavor and tender texture. The steak is marinated in a zesty blend of lime juice, orang, garlic, and cilantro, giving each bite a burst of fresh citrus and savory spice. Grilled to perfection, the meat develops beautiful char marks on the outside while staying juicy and pink inside — the perfect balance of sear and tenderness.

Serve this carne asada sliced thin and sizzling hot. It's delicious on its own, but truly shines in tacos, burritos, or rice bowls topped with avocado, pico de gallo, and a squeeze of lime. Whether you're hosting a backyard cookout or just craving a taste of authentic Mexican street flavor, this recipe promises to deliver pure, mouthwatering satisfaction.

Ingredients

  • Olive Oil.
  • Lime Juice.
  • Orange Juice.
  • White Wine Vinegar. Apple cider vinegar is good too.
  • Chili PeppersUse serrano peppers for a spicier version, jalapenos for a milder version.
  • Garlic and Onion.
  • Fresh Chopped Cilantro
  • Spices.Cumin, oregano, sea salt, black pepper, ancho powder (optional, my personal favorite).
  • Skirt or Flank Steak.You can use other cuts of steak. See below.

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine olive oil, lime juice, orange juice, and white wine vinegar. Whisk until the liquids are well blended.
  2. Add finely chopped chili peppers, minced garlic, diced onion, and fresh chopped cilantro to the marinade. Stir to mix the aromatics evenly.
  3. Season the mixture with cumin, oregano, sea salt, black pepper, and a pinch on ancho chili powder if you'd like a smoky depth of flavor.
  4. Place the skirt or flank steak in alarge resealable bag or shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the meat, making sure it's completely coated.
  5. Seal or cover, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours — ideally overnight — to allow the flavors to soak deep into the steak.
  6. When ready to cook, preheat your grill to high heat (about 450-500°F). Lightly oil the grates to prevent sticking.
  7. Remove the steake from the marinade and let excess liquid drip off. Grill the stea for about 4-6 minutes per side, depending on thickness, until nicely charred on the outside and medium-rare to medium inside.
  8. Transfer the cooked steak to a cutting board and let rest 5-10 minutes so the juices redistribute.
  9. Slice the carne asada thinly against the grain for maximum tenderness.
  10. Serve hot with warm tortillas, fresh lime wedges, pico de gallo, avocado slices, or rice and beans on the side.
