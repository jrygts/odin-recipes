Description

Elote, or Mexican street corn, is a vibrant blend of smoky, creamy, and tangy flavors that capturs the spirit of Mexico's beloved street food. Fresh ears of corn are grilled until the kernels are sweet and charred, creating a perfectcontrast of crisp and tender textures. Once hot off the grill, the corn is slathered with a luscious coating of creamy mayo, crumbled cotija cheese, chili powder, and a splash of lime — a combination that's equal parts savory, spicy, and refreshing.

Served on a stick or right off the cob, elotes are messy in the best possible way — the kind of snack that demands both hands and leaves you smiling with every bite. They're the perfect side to grilled meats like carne asada or a standout treat on their own, bringing color and joy to any table. Whether you're at a backyard cookout or recreating a taste of Mexico's bustling street markets, this elote recipe delivers a burst of flavor that's impossible to resist.

Ingredients

  • 4 ears corn*, husked.
  • 2 Tablespoons mayonnaise.
  • 2 Tablespoons Mexican crema or sour cream.
  • 1/2 cup Cotija cheese, freshly grated, or queso fresco*.
  • Chili Powder, or tajin, to taste.
  • 1 lime, quartered.
  • Fresh Chopped Cilantro, for garnish, optional

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grillto medium-high heat (around 400°F / 200°C). Clean and lightly oil the grates to prevent sticking.
  2. Place the husked corn directly on the grill. Cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are tender and lightly charred on all sides.
  3. While the corn grills, mix the mayonnaise and Mexican crema (or sour cream) together in a small bowl until smooth.
  4. Once the corn is cooked, remove it from the grill and use a brush or spoon to generously coateach ear with mayo-crema mixture while still warm.
  5. Sprinkle the grated Cotija cheese evenly over the corn so it sticks to the creamy coating.
  6. Dust each cob with chili powder or Tajin to your preferred level of heat.
  7. Squeeze fresh lime juice over the top for a bright, tangy finish.
  8. Garnish with chopped cilantro, if desired, and serve immediately while hot.
